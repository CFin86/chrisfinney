The CHALLENGE

You will receive mockups produced by a graphic designer. This site will have the following reqiurements:

  • Must be Responsive with a navbar at the top of the site. (look good on mobile and desktop)
  • A Product List Page - Shows tiles of product images, along with title, short description, and price.
  • A Contact Us Page - Should have a form where people can input their name, email, and a message.
  • You should use SendGrid's API to email yourselves in response to this form being filled out.
  • Use Stripe - Should have fields for email, card number, CVC, expiration, and billing zip code.

A full description of the challenge may be viewed here.

Getting Started

This was a project that was assigned during the start of week 8 of 10 of Covalence Boot Camp. When they announced the team leaders, and to my surprise, I was selected as one, I remember thinking to myself, “Well, this is it, everybody is about to find out exactly how little I know.” To my surprise, though, when we started, not only did we make awesome, incredibly quick progress, my team started asking me how I would approach problems. And even to my bigger surprise, my solutions actually worked! (sometimes) This was one of my first breakthrough moments of the famous Imposter Syndrome. I realized at the end of this project that I can do this. I can be a Full Stack Web Developer.

Responsive Design

The term coined for a website that looks great on a Samsung Galaxy S8, an Apple iPad, a 15” laptop, and everything else in between. In August of 2011, Twitter made my life a whole easier, by creating a framework called Bootstrap. If you look at the image to the right, you’ll see that it’s the same web page as the one in the “Getting Started” section. However, the greeting is rearranged to accommodate a smaller screen size, and also the menu changes to be more intuitive for a mobile device. If you would care to see this in action, and you’re browsing on a laptop or desktop, resize your web browser. All of the content will shift so that it is easier to view.

The Product Page

A Quick Note: I try my best to blog about coding in a simplistic, easy to understand manner, but this section gets pretty nerdy. Feel free to skip it.

It's no secret that I love AngularJS. The two-way binding with ng-model is crazy useful, and that with the built in ngResource model and $resource factory for RESTful service calls is a thing of absolute beauty. Another great feature is ngRepeat. I hate cluttered files, and even more so, I hate repeating myself. ngRepeat gives me the ability to write code for only one item, it uses that code as a template, and then it automatically repeats the information that's stored in my database and updates as a new item! And then theres's UI-Router, I found that I could have multiple views on one page, all with different controllers, it was a game changer. For example, the page you're viewing right now, is actually four .html files on one! Angular is Awesome! Thanks Google!
